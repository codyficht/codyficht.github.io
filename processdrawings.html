Works: Process Drawings

Grey (3)

Detail, acrylic and dry wall, 8ft x 3 ft, 2015.
In this piece one can see brown areas around the remaining white shapes. This is the brown paper sheet placed around the gypsum to hold it in place, that I painted white. One can see slice marks through the remaining white areas and in the gypsum. It has undergone an extensive process as the result some pieces are barely hanging onto the material.

Grey (3)

Acrylic and dry wall, 8ft x 3ft, 2015.
This is the final drywall piece created up until this point in my artistic career. I painted it white in order to reveal the white gypsum more effectively.

Growing Pains

Detail, duct tape, 8 1/2ft x 10ft, 2015.
This is a close up of the last image. If one pressed their hand against the work, the tape would flatten against the wall. It is extremely fragile.

Growing Pains

Duct tape, 8 1/2ft x 10ft, 2015.
This is a piece created by carefully placing duct tape on a wall to create a shadow drawing.

Get The Point

Side view, paper, 22” x 30”, 2015.
This piece needs to be flesh against a surface, with a light source behind it. As a result it was shown in a window.

Get The Point

Paper, 22” x 30”, 2015.
This is a piece made by creating push pin sized holes, from a tool that I fashioned through an en- tire sheet of paper.

Black (2)

Acrylic and dry wall, 3 1/2ft x 7ft, 2015.
This is another dry wall piece made by carving out the gypsum with printmaking tools and x-acto knives.

Red (1)

Detail, acrylic paint and dry wall, 8ft x 4ft, 2014.

Red (1)

Acrylic paint and dry wall, 8ft x 4ft, 2014.
This is a sheet of drywall that I painted red and carved out with printmaking and x-acto knives. The white hue is the areas that have been carved out.

A Touch Of O.C.D

Paper, 72” x 30”, 2013.
This is a roll of wrapping paper that I rubbed back and fourth between my fingers for over twenty four hours. The light red hue on the top half of the roll is where this process begins.

Rub It In

White and black paper, 44” x 30”, 2013.
This is a sheet of paper that I sanded. I placed a black sheet behind it to view the process more efficiently.
